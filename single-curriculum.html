Understanding the Principles and Theory of Visual Design

One of the most difficult parts of talking about the principles of design is figuring out just how many principles there actually are (are there
five? Seven? 10?). And once that’s been figured out, which of these supposed design fundamentals should be included?
Below are what you will learn.

What you’ll Learn.

Contrast

One of the most common complaints designers have about client feedback often revolves around clients who say a design needs to
“pop” more. In this Course you’ll Learn about contrast

Balance

Every element of a design—typography, colors, images, shapes, patterns, etc.—carries a visual weight. Some elements are heavy and draw the eye, while other elements are lighter. The way these elements are laid out
on a page should create a feeling of balance.

Emphasis

Proportion

Hierarchy is another principle of design that directly relates to how well content can be processed by people using a website. The most important elements (or content) should appear to be the most important.

Repetition

Repetition is a great way to reinforce an idea. It’s also a great way to unify a design that brings together a lot of different elements.
